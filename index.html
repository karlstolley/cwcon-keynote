Digital Writing

A Meditation on Difficulty

Question:

What is the intellectual stature of the digital writer? The professor of digital writing? The professional digital writer (the web designer, developer, content strategist, etc.)? -- in an age where, in the United States, the internet is accessed, as of late 2012, by 81% of adults?

The Universal Intellectual

The ‘universal’ intellectual derives from the jurist or notable, and finds his fullest manifestation in the writer...
Foucault, “Truth and Power,” 128

...the threshold of writing, as the sacralizing mark of the [‘universal’] intellectual, has disappeared.
Foucault, “Truth and Power,” 127

...the fact that it was precisely a matter of theory, that he needed scientific credentials...proves that the activity of the writer was no longer the focus of things.
Foucault, “Truth and Power,” 127

The Specific Intellectual

The ‘specific’ intellectual derives from quite another figure, not the jurist or notable, but the savant or expert. Foucault, “Truth and Power,” 128

Oppenheimer

It’s because he [as a ‘specific’ intellectual] had a direct and localized relation to scientific knowledge and institutions that the atomic scientist could make his intervention; but, since the nuclear threat affected the whole human race and the fate of the world, his discourse could at the same time be the discourse of the universal. Foucault, “Truth and Power,” 128

One may even say that the role of the specific intellectual must become more and more important in proportion to the political responsibilities which he is obliged willy-nilly to accept, as a nuclear scientist, computer expert, pharmacologist, etc. Foucault, “Truth and Power,” 130-131

Threshold Concepts

Five Qualities

  1. Transformative
  2. Irreversible
  3. Integrative
  4. Bounded
  5. Troublesome
  6. Discursive

Let's take them one by one

Transformative

Threshold concepts fundamentally alter ways of seeing, thinking, and/or doing.

Irreversible

Once learned, threshold concepts cannot be unlearned, at least without great difficulty. (They can be disregarded, perhaps, but because they are transformative, their effects still linger.)

Integrative

Threshold concepts expose relationships between knowledge that may have previously seemed disparate and unrelated.

Bounded

Threshold concepts open up not to mastery, necessarily, but to additional thresholds, which must in turn be crossed.

Troublesome

Threshold concepts are steeped in "troublesome knowledge": knowledge that is alien, counterintuitive, and potentially aggravating. For this reason, it is often an iterative experience to cross a threshold; learners move back and forth across it.

Discursive

Threshold concepts change the manner and level of sophistication in which learners talk and write.
