The Specific Intellectual

Regarding Oppenheimer

Thesis Statement

Given the opportunity for extended encounters with difficulty (rather than the software tools that route around it), digital writers can become specific intellectuals: people whose deep technological expertise rivals that of their command of rhetoric–who are therefore able to learn, teach, and build things that scare the living crap out of others.

Writing takes a difficult digital turn to restore its status as “the locus of things.”

How to Proceed

  • Identify productive points of difficulty;
  • Refine the conception of difficulty through “threshold concepts”;
  • Describe “threshold concepts” in digital writing through Unix philosophy and The Pragmatic Programmer, as enacted in three different courses;
  • Offer a plan for professional development.

Difficulty is not the opposite of ease

Dreamweaver is difficult. WordPress is difficult. The command line is difficult. All of this is difficult.

Whatever you are doing to write digitally, it’s difficult.

The question is, “What kind of difficulty?”

Threshold Concepts

Five Qualities + One Bonus

  1. Transformative
  2. Irreversible
  3. Integrative
  4. Bounded
  5. Troublesome
  6. BONUS: Discursive

Let’s take them one by one

Using Web standards as a concrete example...

Web standards in 30 seconds

Transformative

Threshold concepts fundamentally alter ways of seeing, thinking, and/or doing.

  • Structure (HTML)
  • Presentation (CSS)
  • Behavior (JavaScript + DOM)

Irreversible

Once learned, threshold concepts cannot be unlearned, at least without great difficulty. (They can be disregarded, perhaps, but because they are transformative, their effects still linger.) 

<body><font face=verdana>
body { font-family: Verdana, Helvetica, sans-serif; }

Integrative

Threshold concepts expose relationships between knowledge that may have previously seemed disparate and unrelated. 

$ cd www
$ ls
foo/
$ ls foo/
bar.html
$ chmod 644 foo/bar.html

<a href="foo/bar.html">

Bounded

Threshold concepts open up not to mastery, necessarily, but to additional thresholds, which must in turn be crossed. 

<a href="http://ext.example.com/" target="_blank">
/*jQuery*/
$('a[href^="http://"]').click(function() {
  window.open(this.href);
  return false;
});

Troublesome

Threshold concepts are steeped in “troublesome knowledge”: knowledge that is alien, counterintuitive, and potentially aggravating. For this reason, it is often an iterative experience to cross a threshold; learners move back and forth across it. 

<writing class="also-writing">More writing.</writing>
writing.also-writing {
  writing: writing;
  writing: writing, writing, writing;
  writing: writing writing;
}

Discursive

Threshold concepts change the manner and level of sophistication in which learners talk and write.
